How to Calculate Tile Grout: The Complete Guide

Calculating tile grout seems straightforward, but most DIYers end up making at least one extra trip to the hardware store because they underestimated how much they need. This guide explains the math behind our calculator, why it works, and the common mistakes that leave you short — or stuck with five extra bags.

The Formula Explained

The volume of grout in a tiled area depends on four measurements: tile length, tile width, tile depth (thickness), and grout gap width (also called the grout joint). Here is the industry-standard formula used by professionals:

Grout Volume = [(L + W) / (L × W)] × D × G × Area × Density

Where L is tile length, W is tile width, D is tile depth, G is grout gap width, and Density is the grout powder density (approximately 1.6 kg/L for sanded grout). This formula works because it calculates the total linear length of grout joints per unit area and multiplies by the cross-sectional area of each joint.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

  1. Determine the grout joint cross-section. This is the gap width multiplied by the tile depth. For a 3/16" gap on a 3/8"-thick tile, the cross-section is 0.1875 × 0.375 = 0.0703 square inches.
  2. Calculate the total length of grout lines per tile. Each tile has grout on two sides (one length side and one width side, since adjacent tiles share joints). Total linear grout per tile = (L + W).
  3. Scale to your area. Divide the total area by the area of one tile (L × W) to get the tile count, then multiply by grout per tile.
  4. Convert volume to weight. Grout is sold by weight. Sanded grout has a density around 100 lbs/ft³ (1.6 kg/L). Unsanded grout is slightly lighter.

Sanded vs. Unsanded Grout

The type of grout you use depends on the width of your gap:

  • Unsanded grout is used for joints 1/8" (3mm) or narrower. It's smoother, easier to work into thin lines, and better for polished stone that could be scratched by sand particles.
  • Sanded grout is used for joints wider than 1/8". The sand provides structural strength and prevents the grout from cracking as it cures in wider gaps.

Pro Tip: If you're tiling a shower floor with small mosaic tiles (1" or 2"), the grout-to-tile ratio is much higher than with large 12" tiles. You may need two to three times more grout per square foot than you'd expect.

Common Mistakes That Waste Grout (and Money)

1. Forgetting the Waste Factor

No matter how careful you are, some grout sticks to your bucket, your float, and gets wiped off during cleaning. Professionals add a 10% waste factor as standard practice. For intricate patterns or first-time tilers, consider 15%.

2. Ignoring Tile Thickness

Many online calculators only ask for gap width and forget tile depth. But grout fills the full depth of the joint — a 1/2" thick tile uses 33% more grout than a 3/8" tile with the same gap width.

3. Not Accounting for Irregular Tiles

Hand-made or natural stone tiles have slight size variations, which means the gap width isn't perfectly uniform. If using irregular tiles, add an extra 5-10% to your estimate.

Watch Out: Never mix more grout than you can apply in 20-30 minutes. Grout begins to set quickly once water is added. Mix in small batches to avoid waste.

How Much Does a Bag of Grout Cover?

A standard 25-pound bag of sanded grout covers approximately:

  • 95-100 sq ft of 12×12" tile with 1/8" joints
  • 50-60 sq ft of 6×6" tile with 1/8" joints
  • 25-35 sq ft of 12×12" tile with 1/4" joints
  • 8-12 sq ft of 2×2" mosaic tile with 1/16" joints

These are rough estimates. Our calculator gives you a precise number based on your exact measurements, so you buy the right amount the first time.

Tips for a Perfect Grout Job

  • Wait 24 hours after laying tiles before grouting. The thinset adhesive needs time to cure.
  • Use a grout float held at a 45-degree angle. Push grout diagonally across joints to fill them completely.
  • Clean as you go with a damp (not wet) sponge. Excess water weakens the grout and causes discoloration.
  • Seal your grout 48-72 hours after it cures. Unsealed grout absorbs moisture and stains easily.
